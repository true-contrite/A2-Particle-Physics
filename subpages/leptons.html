- Leptons -

Like quarks the six leptons are arrangend into pairs, each particle is paired with it's corresponding neutrino. Neutrinos are point like and have no charge so have very little interactions with other particles. A neutrino can pass straight through the Earth without interacting with anything making them very difficult to detect.
Muons and tau are unstable leptons, they don't exist for long since and decay into stable electrons.

Each lepton is given a lepton number of +1 but these values are specific to the different particles.
e.g - if you had one electron the Le number would be +1 but the Lµ and Lτ numbers would be 0 since there aren't any of their corresponding leptons.
